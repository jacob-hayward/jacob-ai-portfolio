**What I did:**

Closed out Phase 1 properly. Closed that thread and kept moving.

International ambition is noted neutrally. The European strategy lives where it belongs — in private.

**What worked:**

Structuring the project document against real enterprise templates from work. It means the portfolio now contains something that looks and reads like genuine organisational AI project experience — because it is. That's a subtle but meaningful signal to anyone reviewing it seriously.

Also worth noting: the two-Claude approach is working well. One session for strategic guidance and documentation, one dedicated build journal partner. Keeps the thinking clean.

**What failed:**

Nothing broke today but I caught myself questioning whether the visa agent was impressive enough — briefly considered pivoting again. Worth documenting honestly because it's a pattern to watch. Ideation is useful until it becomes a way of avoiding the build.

**What I learned:**

The project isn't just about what gets built. It's about demonstrating that I understand the full lifecycle — why each phase exists, what it produces, and how it connects to the next one. A hiring manager who sees someone move from idea, to scope document, to architecture, to build, to failure log, to retrospective is seeing someone who thinks like a project owner. That's the point.

I also caught myself being too harsh about the technical simplicity of the visa agent. Starting a project isn't the moment to judge its ceiling — it's the moment to lay the foundation properly and let the complexity emerge through the build.
